🌸 Who I Am

A reader first.
A writer by necessity.

Beyond writing code, I have been an avid reader since the 4th grade — someone who grew up dog-earing paperbacks and writing in margins long before I knew what an editorial process was.

I write compulsively: essays, cultural commentary, personal pieces rooted in place and identity. I am drawn to storytelling that lives at the intersection of the digital and the dreamed — the niche, the whimsical, the deeply felt.

Currently pursuing a Bachelor of Computer Applications at JCC | JIMS Delhi, I bring an unusual combination of technical literacy and literary sensibility to every piece I touch.

🖊️ Writing Samples

The work that matters.

Four pieces crafted for Dreamworldgirl Zine, plus selected literary essays. ✨

✦ DWG · Cultural Essay
On Cottagecore and the Art of Wanting a Slower Life

Why does the internet keep returning to dewy gardens and bread rising in linen-covered bowls? This essay explores cottagecore not as trend but as collective exhale — the digital generation's most tender act of longing for a pace of life they've never actually lived.

✦ DWG · Personal Essay
Every Book I've Ever Loved Has Ruined Me (A Love Letter)

A zine-style love letter to the specific devastation of a perfect book. With a ranking of personal offenders, unsolicited opinions on reading culture, and a deeply sincere apology to Dostoevsky.

✦ DWG · Cultural Commentary
We Were Girls on the Internet: A Field Guide to Growing Up Online

On growing up on Web 2.0 — the aesthetic language we invented for ourselves on Tumblr and DeviantArt, what it meant to be a girl with a URL before we had a self, and why we're still not over it.

✦ DWG · Personal Essay
The Girl Who Pressed Flowers and Other Things I Romanticise About Myself

An honest, gently funny essay on the gap between the person you imagine you are (journalling by candlelight, pressing wildflowers) and the person you actually are — and why the fantasy might be worth keeping anyway.

✦ Luxury Literature · Essay
The Quiet Luxury of Solitude

On the particular richness of an unobserved hour — the confusion between loneliness and solitude, and why the most radical thing you can do in a world that monetises attention is simply be alone and be entirely fine with it.

✦ Luxury Literature · Essay
On Growing Up Bilingual

Thinking in two languages simultaneously — Hindi for anger and affection, English for thinking about thinking. On the harmonics monolinguals miss, and the self that exists in the other room, speaking differently, meaning the same things.
